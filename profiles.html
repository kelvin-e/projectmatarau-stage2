Georgina Johnson
Tokomaru Bay

Georgina is the Kairangahau for Tokomaru Bay. She has a massive passion for helping people by listening to their views, ideas, suggestions and concerns within the Tairawhiti region, she wants to be useful and helpful. Her degree in Governance and Business is to influence policy, planning and decision-making processes that will have better outcomes for Maori and non-Maori.

She believes as an experienced community representative encouraging, facilitating and supporting effective representation within Ngati Porou is key to supporting quality services and better outcomes for our people. She has been actively involved several local and national networks, but it is more the local groups that she is interested in, these range from children, young adults, elderly, and disabled from all walks of life. She has previously served on the Tangata Whenua Community Research Centre Governance, Tairawhiti District Health Community & Public Health, Health & Disability Multi Region Ethics Committee, CommunityNet Aotearoa, Community Sector Taskforce, National Association OSCAR (Out of School Care & Recreation), Tairawhiti REAP, Tokomaru Bay Community Group Charitable Trust and more recently Alzheimerâ€™s Tairawhiti/Gisborne and now the Gisborne Justice of Peace Association as Registrar.

Michelle Wanoa
Te Araroa

Michelle is the Kairangahau for Te Araroa. She brings to the team a wealth of experience with community engagement and aquaculture research skills. Some projects she has been involved with included data recording re: freshwater and marine sample research for environment for Matahi Marae. She is passionate about her Marae and her community and she can connect with all whanau young and mature. She is a team player and values the opinions of her community. She can work both independently and as a team. She has been involved in several other projects over the years, but most of all she is always keen to get in behind projects such as this to empower whanau. She has proven strengths in presentations, IT, community development research, historical information, coordination & implementation of programs, established networks and knowledge of key people in the Te Tairawhiti rohe.

Merekaraka Te Whitu
Waiapu

Merekaraka is the Kairangahau for Waiapu. She is competent in Te Reo me ona Tikanga o nga Ngati Porou. She has a proven ability to communicate with all age groups. She has well established relationships within all communities along the East Coast. She identifies as a strong team player and has been involved with Community Research Projects in conjunction with Hikurangi Enterprises Ltd & Tikitiki/Rangitukia Township Surveys for GDC She is a valued community member and a good listener.

Waipaina Awarau
Te Puia/Waipiro Bay

Waipaina is the Kairangahau for Waipiro Bay. She has vast experience in environmental research studies. She is an experienced researcher and has also lad projects whilst working in Queensland, Australia. Waipaina has exceptional research skills and has also been a trainer and Instructor in her fields. She also has experience working for State Government in Australia working in remote areas, monitoring, studying and researching for various projects. She has now been home in Waipiro Bay for just over 2 years and is very actively involved in her community. She is committed to sharing her skills and experience for the advancement of all the communities along the East Coast. She has been actively involved in a variety of research reports. She is a massive asset to all our communities and a valued member of this team.

Arlouise Brooking
Wharekahika

Arlouise is the Kairangahau for Potaka/Wharekahika (Hicks Bay). Arlouise has always been an active part of her community even whilst living abroad. She brings a wealth of experience in small business start-up and personal development mentoring. She has the proven ability to inspire others to be the best version of themselves. She has been a motivator for her small community, taking feedback during her current research of Project Matarau and engaging agencies and networks to implement activities and services. She inspires the community and is strongly involved in community development and is not afraid to find ways to make things happen. She has a diploma in accounting through TWOR, which also includes non-financial accounting (community assets) and has completed Iwi and Hapu studies of her community. Arlouise has a proven ability to work independently and as a team member.
